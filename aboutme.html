My Story

I grew up moving. My father served in the US Military and later the State Department, which meant my childhood was spent across continents — five years in New Delhi, time in Addis Ababa, and travel across most of Europe and Asia before most people my age had a passport.

Living abroad teaches you things no classroom can. Adaptability. How to operate in unfamiliar systems. How much precision matters when the stakes are real. During my time in Delhi, I interned across multiple offices at the US Embassy — getting a ground-level view of how large institutions actually function.

That background is why data engineering resonates with me. I've seen what it looks like when information systems work and when they don't, across governments, cultures, and time zones.

Today I work at Apple while finishing my CS degree, where I build software and engineer data systems. I love creating things that work — cleanly, reliably, at scale.
